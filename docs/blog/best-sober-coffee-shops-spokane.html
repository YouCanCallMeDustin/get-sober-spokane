The Best Sober Coffee Shops & Hangouts in Spokane

Safe, warm, alcohol-free spaces to meet a sponsor, read the Big Book, or just exist peacefully.

March 2026  ·  5 min read

When you first give up drinking, evening socializing suddenly feels terrifying. The bars, the breweries, the pubs—so much of Spokane's social scene revolves around alcohol. If you're looking for a comfortable, warm place to meet your sponsor on a Tuesday night or hang out with sober friends on a Saturday devoid of alcohol triggers, coffee shops are your new best friend. Here are some of the best (and late-night) sober coffee spots in town.

The Late-Night Classics

The hardest hours in early sobriety are usually between 8 PM and midnight when the cravings hit the hardest and the meetings end. Finding a coffee shop open late is a massive win.

  • First Avenue Coffee (Downtown): A beautifully modern, bright location in the heart of downtown. With excellent pour-overs, massive windows, and plenty of seating, it's a stellar spot for meeting up after a downtown AA or NA meeting.
  • Atticus Coffee & Gifts (Downtown): A staple of the Spokane coffee scene. It's cozy, slightly dark, famously welcoming, and attached to a wonderful gift shop. If you want somewhere to sit quietly and read recovery literature, this is the spot.

The Neighborhood Hideaways

Want something away from the hustle of downtown? Spokane's neighborhoods are packed with local roasters.

  • Indaba Coffee (Multiple Locations): Indaba is a local powerhouse with locations in Kendall Yards, Riverside, and Broadway. The Kendall Yards location sits right on the Centennial Trail—perfect for grabbing a coffee and taking a sober walk along the river.
  • Meeting House Café (South Hill): Located up on the South Hill, this cafe literally operates as a community gathering space. It has large tables perfect for group meetings or sitting down with a sponsor.
  • Brews Bros Coffee Bar (Brownes Addition): A cozy, deeply atmospheric spot in Spokane's oldest neighborhood. It is highly walkable and nestled among beautiful historic homes.

The "Not A Coffee Shop, But Caffeinated" Option

Sometimes you just need somewhere 24/7 or highly casual.

  • The Shari's on Monroe or Sullivan: In the recovery world, there is a long-standing tradition of hitting a local diner after a late-night meeting. Ordering a slice of pie and endless cups of decaf at a classic diner booth is practically a rite of passage in the Spokane sober community. No one cares how long you sit there, and it's totally alcohol-free.

Looking for Sober Events?

You don't have to just sit in coffee shops! Spokane has a vibrant, active sober community that hosts regular outdoor hikes, dinners, and events.
