The Ultimate Spokane Recovery Guide

Your comprehensive directory to addiction recovery and support services in Spokane, WA

Navigating the journey to sobriety can be overwhelming, especially when trying to find the right local resources. This definitive guide connects you with essential addiction recovery resources across Spokane—from immediate emergency help to long-term sober living solutions.

1. Emergency Help in Spokane

If you or a loved one is experiencing a life-threatening medical emergency or overdose, call 911 immediately. For mental health crises or urgent addiction support, these local resources are available:

First Call for Help (Crisis Line)

Phone: (509) 838-4428

Provides 24/7 crisis intervention and referral services for mental health and substance abuse emergencies in Spokane County.

Spokane Regional Health District (Opioid Overdose Prevention)

Offers resources on recognizing overdoses and obtaining Naloxone (Narcan) to reverse opioid overdoses locally.

2. Detox Centers in Spokane

Medical detoxification is often the first vital step in recovery. Spokane offers professional medical settings to safely manage withdrawal symptoms.

View our complete guide to Detox Centers in Spokane to learn about options for medical, social, and outpatient detox programs.

3. Rehab Centers & Addiction Treatment

Inpatient and outpatient rehab facilities provide the critical foundation for long-term sobriety.

Spokane County Counseling and Recovery Services

Offers accessible substance use disorder assessments, case management, and structured treatment programs tailored for Spokane residents.

Explore our comprehensive Addiction Treatment guide for detailed information on residential rehab, intensive outpatient programs (IOP), and specialized therapies.

4. AA Meetings (Alcoholics Anonymous)

Peer support is the backbone of recovery for millions. Spokane hosts a robust network of AA meetings every day of the week.

Find AA Meetings in Spokane —Discover schedules, locations (including the Spokane Alano Club), and what to expect at your first meeting.

5. NA Meetings (Narcotics Anonymous)

For those recovering from drug addiction, NA provides a welcoming fellowship of individuals sharing their experience, strength, and hope.

Find NA Meetings in Spokane —Browse daily meeting times and locations across Spokane County.

6. Counseling & Mental Health

Treating addiction often requires addressing co-occurring mental health disorders (dual diagnosis).

Professional therapists and specialized clinics in Spokane offer Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), and individual counseling designed specifically for addiction recovery. Visit our Medical & Mental Health section for providers.

7. Sober Housing & Transitional Living

Transitioning from rehab back into the community is significantly safer when living in a structured, drug-and-alcohol-free environment.

Read our guide on Sober Living in Spokane to learn about local Oxford Houses, halfway houses, and recovery residences.

8. Community Support: Recovery Café Spokane

Recovery Café Spokane

Recovery Café provides a beautiful, safe, and supportive community space for individuals who have been traumatized by substance use, homelessness, and other mental health challenges. It's a place to find community, attend recovery circles, share meals, and build a new life in sobriety.
