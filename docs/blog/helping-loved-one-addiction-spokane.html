How to Help a Loved One with Addiction in Spokane

Addiction is a family disease. Here is how you can help them—and protect yourself.

March 2026  ·  7 min read

Watching a child, a spouse, or a best friend spiral into addiction is one of the most terrifying, deeply exhausting experiences a person can endure. You likely feel guilty, angry, entirely out of control, and desperate for a solution. The hard truth is that you cannot cure your loved one's addiction. But you *can* change how you interact with it, learn to protect your own sanity, and guide them toward the Spokane-area resources that can actually help them when they are ready.

1. Stop Enabling (Boundaries are Love)

The line between "helping" and "enabling" is painfully thin. Enabling happens when we step in to shield the addict from the consequences of their drinking or drug use. Examples include:

  • Paying their rent when they spent their paycheck on drugs.
  • Calling in sick to their boss because they are too hungover to work.
  • Bailing them out of the Spokane County Jail.

In the recovery world, it's often said that an addict won't change until the pain of staying the same becomes greater than the pain of changing. By absorbing their consequences, you inadvertently prolong their addiction. Setting boundaries (e.g., "I love you, but you cannot stay in my house if you are using") feels cruel in the short term, but it is lifesaving in the long term.

2. Find Your Own Support Group locally

You cannot pour from an empty cup. Just as alcoholics go to AA, families of alcoholics need their own support to learn how to cope with the trauma of active addiction.

  • Al-Anon & Nar-Anon: These are free, 12-step support groups specifically for the friends and families of alcoholics and addicts. There are dozens of Al-Anon meetings happening every week across Spokane, Spokane Valley, and Coeur d'Alene. You will meet people who know *exactly* what you are going through.
  • SMART Recovery Family & Friends: A science-based alternative to Al-Anon that focuses on teaching CRAFT (Community Reinforcement and Family Training) skills—proven methods to communicate effectively with an addicted loved one without fighting.

3. Carry Naloxone (Narcan)

If your loved one is using heroin, fentanyl, or any illicit pills in the Spokane area, they are at massive risk for a fatal overdose. Fentanyl is in our local drug supply.

You can walk into almost any pharmacy in Washington State and obtain Naloxone (Narcan) without a prescription. It is a safe, easy-to-use nasal spray that reverses opioid overdoses in seconds. Having it in your medicine cabinet or purse could absolutely save their life while you wait for the paramedics.

4. Gather Information, Don't Force It

You can't force an adult into rehab. But when they have that brief moment of clarity and say, *"I need help,"* that window of willingness closes fast. Do the homework for them in advance.

Use the **Get Sober Spokane Directory** to find a few treatment centers that accept their insurance. Write down the intake phone numbers. Know where the local detox center is. Have the information ready so that when they finally ask, you can hand it to them immediately.
