A Guide to Second-Chance Employers in Spokane

Rebuilding your career in early recovery, especially with a criminal background.

March 2026  ·  7 min read

Getting sober is the hardest part, but finding a job when your resume has massive gaps—or when a background check reveals a criminal history—can feel impossible. Returning to the workforce in early recovery requires a specific strategy, but finding "felony-friendly" or "second-chance" work in Spokane is entirely possible.

What is a "Second-Chance" Employer?

A second-chance employer actively commits to hiring individuals with criminal records or significant resume gaps resulting from substance abuse or incarceration. These companies recognize that a person's past mistakes do not dictate their future reliability.

In Spokane, second-chance employment largely breaks down into three major industries: Manufacturing/Warehousing, Construction/Trades, and the rapidly growing Hospitality/Culinary sector.

Industries that Commonly Hire in Spokane

  • Trades and Construction: Local unions (IBEW, Carpenters, Plumbers) consistently evaluate applicants on their literal ability to show up sober and do hard work, rather than dwelling heavily on past drug offenses. The trades offer incredibly lucrative, union-protected careers.
  • Logistics & Warehousing: Warehouses (like local distribution centers for major retailers) often hire immediately with very lenient background restrictions, as long as the applicant can consistently pass random UAs.
  • Non-Profits and Peer Support: If you have significant "clean time," your lived experience is highly valuable! Spokane treatment centers, such as Pioneer Human Services, frequently hire Certified Peer Counselors (CPCs) who explicitly have a history of addiction or incarceration.

Free Job Hunt Resources in Spokane

Rather than blindly sending out resumes on Indeed and holding your breath during the background check, utilize organizations in Spokane that exist entirely to get you hired.

  • WorkSource Spokane: Stop by the WorkSource center on Arthur Street. They host regular free workshops on how to answer the dreaded *"tell me about this gap on your resume"* question and how to handle a background check conversation gracefully.
  • Career Path Services: This incredible local non-profit specifically focuses on finding transitional or permanent employment for individuals re-entering society post-incarceration or post-rehab.
  • Goodwill Industries of the Inland Northwest: They do *far* more than retail! Goodwill has robust, free workforce and training programs explicitly for people facing significant barriers to employment.

The Interview: Owning Your Story

If an employer is going to run a background check, get ahead of it. Trying to hide a non-violent drug conviction only guarantees you won't get hired when it inevitably appears. Instead, own it briefly and completely pivot to the present.

**Example:** *"You will notice a gap in my employment between 2023 and 2025 as well as a misdemeanor on my record. During that time, I was dealing with a severe, untreated medical issue. I have since completed rigorous treatment, am in active recovery, and I am highly motivated to be the most reliable worker on your team."*
