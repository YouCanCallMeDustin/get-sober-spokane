20 Sober Things to Do in Spokane, WA – Fun for Every Season

Recovery doesn't mean giving up fun. Spokane is packed with incredible sober experiences — here are 20 to get you started.

March 2026  ·  6 min read

One of the biggest fears people have about getting sober is losing their social life — and that fear makes sense. But here's what nobody tells you: sober life in Spokane can be genuinely, surprisingly, joyfully full. This city has mountains, rivers, world-class running events, art galleries, coffee shops, farmers markets, and a recovery community that knows how to celebrate life. Whether you're brand new to sobriety or years into your journey, these 20 ideas will help you rediscover just how much fun doesn't require a drink.

1. Outdoor Adventures

Spokane is an outdoor lover's dream, with easy access to rivers, mountains, parks, and trails no matter the season.

Riverfront Park

Walk the falls, ride the historic gondola over Spokane Falls, or catch a summer concert in the park — all completely free.

Centennial Trail

A 37-mile paved trail perfect for walking, running, or cycling along the Spokane River from downtown to Coeur d'Alene.

Mount Spokane State Park

Ski and snowboard in winter, hike and mountain bike in summer. Only 30 minutes from downtown.

Manito Park & Gardens

Five distinct gardens including a Japanese garden and rose garden — stunning in spring and summer. Free admission always.

2. Arts & Culture

Spokane's arts scene is deeper than most people expect — and seeing it through sober eyes makes the experience that much richer.

  • Northwest Museum of Arts & Culture (MAC): Rotating exhibits covering art, history, and Native American culture. One of the most visited museums in Washington.
  • First Friday Art Walk: Every first Friday of the month, galleries and studios across Spokane open their doors for free. Great for meeting creative people.
  • Spokane Civic Theatre: Professional community theater productions year-round. Volunteer usher to attend shows for free.
  • INB Performing Arts Center: Major touring shows, concerts, and performances. Check the calendar for free or low-cost events.
  • Spokane Symphony: Offers community concerts and open rehearsals at significantly reduced prices throughout the season.

3. Community Events

Spokane hosts world-famous events that bring the whole city together — alcohol is never the point.

  • Bloomsday (May): One of the largest road races in the US — 50,000+ runners. Sign up, train with a running club, and cross that finish line.
  • Hoopfest (June): The world's largest 3-on-3 basketball tournament. Play in it, volunteer, or just watch. Downtown Spokane transforms for an entire weekend.
  • Spokane Farmers Market (May–Oct): Every Wednesday and Saturday downtown. Fresh produce, local food, live music, and friendly faces.
  • Spokane Pride (June): A welcoming, inclusive community celebration with a parade, vendors, and live entertainment.
  • Spokane County Interstate Fair (September): Rides, live music, food, and exhibits — a classic family-friendly outing.

4. Fitness & Wellness

Physical health and recovery are deeply connected. Spokane's fitness community is welcoming and often free or low-cost.

  • Spokane Running Club: Free group runs through the city. Beginner-friendly, no experience required.
  • Yoga in the Park: Free community yoga sessions at Riverfront Park and Manito Park during summer months.
  • Spokane Valley Recreation Centers: Low-cost drop-in fitness, swimming, and group classes at multiple locations.
  • Inland NW Hiking: An active community hiking group with weekly outings ranging from easy to strenuous — check their Facebook group.
  • YMCA of the Inland Northwest: Sliding-scale memberships available for those in financial need. Full gym, pool, and group classes.
Exercise is one of the most effective natural mood boosters — and it's free. Even a 20-minute walk can shift how you feel.

5. Volunteer & Give Back

Giving your time to others in recovery — or to the broader Spokane community — is one of the most fulfilling things you can do in sobriety.

  • AA / NA Service Work: Set up chairs, make coffee, become a group secretary, or lead a meeting. Service is central to the 12-step philosophy for a reason.
  • Union Gospel Mission: Volunteer in the kitchen, serve meals, and connect with people who are exactly where you once were.
  • Second Harvest Food Bank: Sort and distribute food to thousands of families each week. A grounding, community-centered experience.
  • Young People in Recovery (YPR): Volunteer as an advocate or event coordinator for Spokane's young recovery community.
  • Habitat for Humanity Spokane: Build homes, build skills, and build friendships — weekend build days are open to all skill levels.

6. Sober Social Spots

Sometimes you just want to hang out somewhere comfortable. These Spokane spots are naturally low-key and alcohol-free by default.

  • Atticus Coffee & Gifts: A cozy, artsy coffee shop in the South Perry District — great for reading, meeting people, or journaling.
  • Auntie's Bookstore: Spokane's beloved independent bookstore. Browse for hours, attend a reading, or join a book club.
  • Pinball Pete's & Arcade Bars (non-alcohol focus): Spokane has a growing arcade and board game scene — great for social events that aren't bar-focused.
  • Spokane Public Library — Central & Branches: Free community programs, reading rooms, and quiet spaces to decompress and learn.
  • Rockwood Coffee: Another beloved neighborhood café known for its welcoming regulars and relaxed atmosphere.

7. Recovery Community Events

The recovery community in Spokane hosts its own lineup of sober social events, celebrations, and meetups throughout the year.

  • Recovery Month (September): Free public events across the city celebrating recovery — walks, rallies, picnics, and speaker panels.
  • Sober Sports Leagues: Recreational volleyball, softball, and disc golf leagues run by and for the recovery community.
  • Speaker Meetings & Step Workshops: Open to anyone. Inspiring and often unexpectedly entertaining evenings hosted by local AA/NA groups.
  • Get Sober Spokane Community Events: We share sober-friendly events and activities curated specifically for the Spokane recovery community.
