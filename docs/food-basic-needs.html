Hot Meal Programs & Food Delivery

Beyond food banks, Spokane offers free hot meals and home-delivered nutrition programs for vulnerable populations.

Shalom Ministries – Dining with Dignity

Free, nutritious meals in a dignified downtown environment. No judgment, just good food and community connection.

  • Address: 518 W 3rd Ave, Spokane, WA 99201
  • Breakfast: Mon–Thu 7:30–8:30 AM
  • Lunch: Mon–Thu 11 AM – 12 PM
  • Community Dinner: Mon 4 & 5 PM
  • Open to anyone hungry (no weapons/drugs/alcohol)
  • shalomministries.org
Meals on Wheels Greater Spokane County

Home-delivered hot meals for seniors (60+) and homebound adults. Includes wellness checks and social contact.

  • Phone: (509) 456-6597
  • Deliveries: Mon–Fri Mid-day
  • Requirement: Age 60+ or homebound with doctor note
  • mowgsc.org
New Hope Ranch Food Ministry

Community outreach providing regular food distribution and warm meals to Hillyard residents.

  • Address: 2524 E Queen Ave, Spokane, WA 99217
  • Hours: Mon & Wed 12–2 PM; Fri 5–6 PM
  • Open to all
Spokane Humane Society Pet Food Pantry

Helps keep pets with their families by providing free dog and cat food to owners in temporary financial need.

  • Address: 6607 N Havana St, Spokane, WA 99217
  • Phone: (509) 467-5235
  • Hours: Tue–Sat 10:30 AM – 6 PM
  • spokanehumanesociety.org

Government Assistance & Additional Resources

In addition to food banks, Spokane offers mobile meal programs and government assistance for food insecurity.

  • Second Harvest Mobile Market: Free produce, meats, and groceries at rotating locations. No appointment needed. Check their website for the next event.
  • Basic Food (SNAP): Apply for monthly food benefits at DSHS Basic Food . Eligibility based on income and household size.
  • Washington 211: Call 2-1-1 or (866) 904-9060 for referrals to food, housing, and emergency services.
  • Grad & Go Meals: Free grab-and-go meals for children and teens through Spokane Public Schools.

Other Basic Needs

Many food banks and community organizations also provide clothing, hygiene products, and utility assistance.

  • Our Place Spokane: Food, clothing, hygiene, utility help, and bus passes at 1509 W College Ave.
  • Salvation Army: Emergency food, clothing, and community services at 204 E Indiana Ave.
  • Spokane Resource Center: Childcare, energy assistance, and wellness programs at spokaneresourcecenter.org .

Access & Support

  • Call Washington 2-1-1 at 2-1-1 or (866) 904-9060 for immediate referrals to food and basic needs services.
  • Visit dshs.wa.gov to apply for SNAP benefits.
  • Browse our full Resource Directory for all verified Spokane recovery resources.

Need Food or Essentials Now?

Call Washington 2-1-1 or visit a local food bank for immediate help.
