Free Addiction Help in Spokane, WA – 15 Resources You Didn't Know Existed

Help is closer than you think — and it's free.

March 2026  ·  7 min read

Asking for help is one of the hardest things anyone can do — especially when you're not sure where to start or whether you can afford it. The truth is, Spokane has a remarkably wide network of free addiction recovery resources that most people never hear about. Whether you need a sponsor, a safe place to sleep, food for the week, or simply someone to talk to at midnight, there is something here for you. This guide covers 15 of the best free resources available right now — no insurance required, no judgment.

1. Peer Support & Sponsor Matching

One of the most powerful (and completely free) tools in early recovery is having someone who has been there walk alongside you. A sponsor isn't a therapist — they're a real person in recovery who shows up, listens, and holds you accountable.

  • Get Sober Spokane Sponsor Finder: Our free sponsor matching tool connects you with local volunteers in Spokane who are ready to support your journey.   Find a sponsor →
  • AA & NA Fellowship: Both programs offer free sponsor connections at any meeting. Walk in, introduce yourself, and ask.
  • SMART Recovery: A secular, science-based alternative to 12-step programs with free local and online meetings.
Tip: You don't have to commit to a sponsor on day one. Many people simply attend a few meetings and find someone whose story resonates.

2. Free Treatment Programs

Cost should never be a barrier to getting treatment. Spokane has several programs that offer free or sliding-scale outpatient and inpatient treatment — many funded through state and federal grants.

  • TRIUMPH Treatment Services: Offers free and low-cost residential treatment for adults.
  • Frontier Behavioral Health: Provides substance use treatment on a sliding fee scale — meaning if you have no income, your cost may be $0.
  • Washington Recovery Help Line: Call 1-866-789-1511 for free 24/7 crisis support and referrals to local treatment.
  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 — free, confidential, 24/7 referral service.

Explore our full list of treatment resources:   Addiction Treatment & Support in Spokane →

3. Emergency Shelter & Sober Living

Stable housing is often the first step toward stable recovery. Many people don't realize that Spokane has multiple emergency shelter options and long-term sober living houses — several of which are free or funded through the county.

  • Union Gospel Mission: Provides free emergency shelter, meals, and a long-term recovery program with housing.
  • Crosswalk Youth Shelter: Free emergency shelter for youth ages 13–17 experiencing homelessness.
  • Volunteers of America: Offers transitional housing and sober living options for adults in recovery.
  • Catholic Charities of Eastern Washington: Emergency rental assistance and housing placements.

See the full shelter and housing resource list:   Emergency Shelter & Sober Living in Spokane →

4. Food & Basic Needs

When you're focused on recovery, having food, clothing, and hygiene supplies shouldn't be another thing to worry about. Spokane's network of food banks and mutual aid organizations makes sure it doesn't have to be.

  • Second Harvest Food Bank: One of the largest food banks in the region — free groceries, no appointment needed at many locations.
  • Spokane County Community Services: Connects residents with emergency food, utility assistance, and clothing vouchers.
  • Catholic Charities Food Pantry: Open to anyone, regardless of faith background.
  • Community Minded Enterprises: Offers a wide range of basic needs support including clothing and hygiene items.

Browse the complete food and basic needs directory:   Food & Basic Needs Resources in Spokane →

5. Mental Health Services

Addiction and mental health are deeply connected. Up to half of people with a substance use disorder also experience a co-occurring mental health condition — and treating both at the same time dramatically improves outcomes.

  • Frontier Behavioral Health: Sliding-scale mental health and substance use treatment — includes crisis services.
  • WA State Mental Health Crisis Line: Call or text 988 for free 24/7 crisis support.
  • Open Door Community Health Centers: Offers integrated mental health and medical care on a sliding fee scale.
  • NAMI Spokane: Free peer support groups and mental health education for individuals and families.

Explore our mental health resource directory:   Mental Health & Medical Services in Spokane →

6. Employment & Skills Training

Rebuilding your career is a huge part of rebuilding your life. Spokane offers free workforce development programs specifically designed for people in recovery or returning from incarceration.

  • WorkSource Spokane: Free job placement assistance, resume help, and skills training funded by the state.
  • Goodwill Industries of the Inland Northwest: Free job training and employment services for people facing barriers.
  • Career Path Services: Employment services for individuals experiencing homelessness or leaving incarceration.
  • Spokane Community College Workforce: Free and low-cost vocational training programs — financial aid available.

See all employment and skills resources:   Employment & Skills Training in Spokane →

7. Community & Sober Activities

Recovery isn't just about stopping — it's about building a life worth living. Spokane has a thriving sober social scene with free and low-cost events, sports leagues, and peer-led community spaces.

  • Young People in Recovery (YPR) Spokane: Free sober social events and advocacy for ages 18–35.
  • Spokane's Sober Sports Leagues: Recreational leagues (volleyball, softball, disc golf) designed for the recovery community.
  • Community Recovery Month Events: Free public events every September celebrating the recovery community.
  • Parks & Outdoors Programs: Spokane's Riverside State Park and trail systems offer free hiking, biking, and group walk events.

Discover sober-friendly community events and activities:   Community & Sober Activities in Spokane →

8. Online Community & Live Chat

Sometimes you need support at 2 AM when no meeting is running and you don't want to call a family member. That's exactly what our online community is here for.

  • Get Sober Spokane Live Chat: Connect with peers in real time — no sign-up required to read, easy to join.   Join the live chat →
  • Get Sober Spokane Community Forum: Post questions, share your story, read what others in Spokane are going through, and find encouragement.   Browse the forum →
  • Reddit r/stopdrinking & r/AlAnon: Large anonymous online communities offering round-the-clock peer support.
  • In The Rooms: Free online 12-step meetings held every hour, every day.
All Get Sober Spokane community spaces are moderated and free. No insurance, no appointments, no judgment.

You Don't Have to Figure This Out Alone

Spokane's recovery community is one of the most generous in the Pacific Northwest. Whether you're on day one or year ten, there is a resource, a person, or a program here for exactly where you are right now.
