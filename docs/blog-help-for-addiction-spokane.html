Where to Get Help for Addiction in Spokane

Published on: October 24, 2024 • 5 min read

Realizing that you or a loved one needs help with drug or alcohol addiction is a courageous and terrifying first step. The good news is that Spokane, WA, offers a wide array of resources dedicated to helping individuals achieve and maintain sobriety. Whether you are dealing with a crisis right now or looking into long-term treatment options, this guide outlines exactly where to turn.

1. Immediate Crisis and Emergency Help

If you are facing a life-threatening emergency, such as a suspected drug overdose, call 911 immediately . Washington State has a Good Samaritan Law that protects individuals from drug possession charges if they seek medical assistance in overdose situations.

For immediate mental health crises or urgent substance use intervention, you can contact the Spokane County Regional Behavioral Health (First Call for Help) hotline at (509) 838-4428. They provide crisis intervention 24/7.

2. Medical Detoxification Centers

For many, the first step to recovery is safely removing drugs or alcohol from their system. Depending on the substance, withdrawing without medical supervision can be extremely dangerous.

Spokane has several specialized facilities offering inpatient, medically supervised detox programs . These centers ensure you are safe, comfortable, and prepared to transition into dedicated treatment.

3. Inpatient and Outpatient Rehab Facilities

Once detox is completed, entering a structured treatment program is vital to avoid relapse.

  • Inpatient Rehab: Best for severe addictions. Patients live at the facility 24/7, participating in rigorous therapy, group counseling, and educational sessions.
  • Outpatient Rehab (IOP/PHP): Ideal for individuals with moderate addictions or those who have completed inpatient care. You attend therapy sessions during the day but return home (or to a sober living house) at night.

Explore top-rated addiction treatment centers in Spokane to find a program that fits your insurance and clinical needs.

4. Local Support Groups (AA & NA)

Long-term recovery relies heavily on community and peer support. You are not alone in this fight.

  • Alcoholics Anonymous (AA): With meetings happening multiple times a day across Spokane, you can always find a supportive group. Find a local AA meeting here .
  • Narcotics Anonymous (NA): Provides a fellowship for individuals recovering from drug addiction. Find a local NA meeting here .

5. Sober Housing Options

Returning to an old living environment full of triggers is a leading cause of relapse. Sober living homes (like Oxford Houses) provide drug-and-alcohol-free transitional housing where you can live with peers who are also committed to recovery. They offer structure, accountability, and safety.

Conclusion

Help is available in Spokane, and recovery is absolutely possible. By reaching out to professional rehab centers, utilizing local crisis lines, and joining community support groups, you can build a strong foundation for a healthier, sober life. Don't wait—take the first step today.
