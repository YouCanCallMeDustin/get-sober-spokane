AA & NA Meetings in Spokane, WA – Complete Guide

Find meeting times, locations, and formats across every Spokane neighborhood.

March 2025  ·  5 min read

What Are AA and NA Meetings?

Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) and Narcotics Anonymous (NA) are free, peer-led support groups for people in recovery from alcohol and drug addiction. Meetings are open to anyone at any stage of recovery — no appointment or commitment required.

Both programs follow a 12-step model built on fellowship, personal accountability, and spiritual (not religious) growth. AA focuses on alcohol; NA focuses on all substances. Many people attend both.

How to Find an AA or NA Meeting in Spokane

Spokane has a robust recovery community with dozens of meetings held every day of the week. Here's how to find one:

  • AA Spokane District: Visit   aa-eastern-wa.org for a full searchable meeting schedule.
  • NA Inland Northwest: Visit   inlandnorthwestna.org for NA meeting listings in Spokane and surrounding areas.
  • SAMHSA Helpline: Call   1-800-662-4357 (free, confidential) for local referral to meetings and treatment.

AA & NA Meetings by Spokane Neighborhood

South Hill

South Hill hosts several daily meetings at local churches and community centers. Popular formats include Big Book study, speaker meetings, and open discussion.

  • Holy Family Church – Monday/Wednesday/Friday evenings
  • South Hill Community Center – Daily noon meetings
  • St. John's Cathedral – Saturday and Sunday morning meetings

North Spokane

North Spokane has a strong AA presence with multiple early morning and evening options throughout the week.

  • First Presbyterian Church – Monday through Saturday mornings
  • North Side Community Hall – Tuesday and Thursday evenings
  • Shadle Park area – Weekend speaker meetings

Spokane Valley

Spokane Valley offers accessible meeting locations near Liberty Lake and the Valley corridor.

  • Valley 12-Step Club – Daily meetings (multiple formats)
  • Liberty Lake Community Church – Wednesday and Friday evenings
  • Greenacres area – Weekend Big Book meetings

Tips for First-Timers

You don't have to speak

First-timers can simply listen. Introduce yourself only if you feel comfortable.

Meetings are confidential

What's shared in the room stays in the room.

Try a few different ones

Each meeting has its own personality. Don't judge all meetings by one.

It's free, always

AA and NA never charge for attendance. A small voluntary donation is sometimes passed.
