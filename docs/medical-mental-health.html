Medical & Mental Health Services in Spokane

Spokane offers a comprehensive network of hospitals, clinics, and mental health providers for individuals and families. Services include emergency care, primary care, behavioral health, dental, and crisis support, with many options for low-income and uninsured residents.

Other Health Services

  • Dental Care: CHAS Health and ABCD (for children under 6 with Medicaid) offer dental services. See srhd.org for more.
  • Vision Care: Likely available at CHAS Health and other clinics.
  • Women’s Health: Providence Holy Family Hospital and other facilities offer women’s health services.
  • Children’s Health: Pediatric care at Providence Sacred Heart and Children’s Hospital.
  • Veterans’ Health: VA Spokane Health Care System and Mann Grandstaff VA Medical Center.
  • Public Health: Spokane Regional Health District offers naloxone training, Nurse-Family Partnership, and more.
  • In-Home Urgent Care: DispatchHealth provides same-day medical care at home ( dispatchhealth.com ).
  • Help Applying for Services: Washington Connection ( waconnection.org ) and Parent Help 123 (1-800-322-2588, parenthelp123.org ) assist with health insurance and more.

Access & Support

  • Call Washington 2-1-1 at (877) 211-9274 for referrals to medical, mental health, and community services.
  • Explore the Fig Tree Resource Guide for a comprehensive directory of health and wellness resources.
  • Use the SAMHSA Treatment Locator to find additional mental health and substance abuse treatment options.
  • Use the Spokane Resource Google Map to locate hospitals, clinics, and mental health services.

Need Medical or Mental Health Support?

Call Washington 2-1-1 or reach out to a local provider for immediate help.
