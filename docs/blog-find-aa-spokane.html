How to Find AA Meetings in Spokane

Published on: October 26, 2024 • 4 min read

If you have decided to stop drinking and are seeking support, Alcoholics Anonymous (AA) is one of the most accessible and proven recovery resources available. Spokane boasts a thriving recovery community with AA meetings held every single day, morning through night, across the entire county. Here is how you can find the right AA meeting for you in Spokane.

Why Attend an AA Meeting?

AA provides a fellowship of individuals who share their experiences, strengths, and hopes with each other to solve their common problem and help others recover from alcoholism. The only requirement for membership is a desire to stop drinking. Meetings offer an unparalleled level of peer support and accountability that is difficult to find anywhere else.

1. Use Our Spokane AA Directory

We have created a dedicated resource to help you locate meetings near you. Visit our Spokane AA Meetings Guide to find a comprehensive breakdown of what to expect, the differences between open and closed meetings, and links to local meeting finders.

2. The Official Spokane Intergroup

The most authoritative and up-to-date source for meeting times and locations is the local AA Intergroup office. The Spokane AA Intergroup website maintains a daily, searchable meeting schedule. You can filter meetings by:

  • Day of the week
  • Time of day (morning, noon, evening, or late-night)
  • Location/Neighborhood (Downtown, Spokane Valley, South Hill, North Side)
  • Meeting Type (Open, Closed, Men's, Women's, LGBTQ+, Big Book Study, Speaker)

3. Download the Meeting Guide App

Brought to you by Alcoholics Anonymous World Services, the "Meeting Guide" app is a free mobile app for iOS and Android. It uses your phone's location to instantly show you the next available AA meetings near you in Spokane. It provides directions, meeting formats, and contact information.

4. Visit the Spokane Alano Club

The Alano Club in Spokane is a dedicated safe space for 12-step recovery groups. They host numerous AA (and Al-Anon) meetings throughout the week. Dropping by a recovery clubhouse is a great way to meet sober individuals and get a physical schedule of local meetings.

What to Expect at Your First Spokane Meeting

Walking into your first AA room can be intimidating. Here are a few things to keep in mind:

  • You don't have to speak. You are completely welcome to sit, listen, and observe.
  • Look for Open Meetings. If you are unsure if you are an alcoholic, an "Open" meeting allows anyone (including family or observers) to attend. "Closed" meetings are strictly for those who have a desire to stop drinking.
  • Anonymity is key. The foundation of AA is privacy. What is said in the rooms stays in the rooms.
