Addiction Therapy in Spokane, WA: Types, Costs, and How to Find Help

Therapy isn't just about talking—it's about rewiring behaviors and healing the root causes of addiction.

March 2026  ·  7 min read

Deciding to seek therapy for addiction is a massive step forward. But for many in Spokane—and everywhere else—the next questions are often overwhelming: What kind of therapy do I need? How much does it cost? Where do I even start looking? Addiction therapy (often called substance abuse counseling) goes far beyond just "talking about your problems." It equips you with concrete tools to handle cravings, manage triggers, and address the underlying mental health issues that often accompany addiction. In this guide, we break down what addiction therapy looks like in Spokane, WA.

1. Types of Addiction Therapy Available in Spokane

Not all therapy is the same. Finding the right "modality" (the specific psychological approach) is crucial for long-term recovery. Here are the most common and effective types of therapy you'll find offered by Spokane-area clinicians:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT): The gold standard for addiction treatment. CBT helps you identify negative thought patterns that lead to substance use and teaches you how to change those patterns. You'll learn highly practical coping skills for when cravings hit.
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT): Originally designed for borderline personality disorder, DBT is incredibly effective for addiction. It focuses on emotional regulation, distress tolerance (getting through a crisis without making it worse), and mindfulness.
  • Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR): If your substance use is tied to past trauma or PTSD, EMDR is a specialized therapy designed to help your brain reprocess traumatic memories so they no longer trigger the urge to use. Many specialized therapists in Spokane offer EMDR.
  • Motivational Interviewing (MI): Often used early in recovery, this approach helps resolve ambivalence. If you're on the fence about whether you *really* want to get sober, MI helps you find your own internal motivation to change.

2. Free and Low-Cost Therapy Options in Spokane

One of the biggest myths about therapy is that it's only for the wealthy. If you are uninsured, underinsured, or on Medicaid (Apple Health), Spokane has a robust network of providers.

  • Frontier Behavioral Health: One of Spokane's largest community mental health providers. They accept Apple Health (Medicaid) and offer services on a sliding fee scale based on your income. They provide both mental health counseling and substance use treatment.
  • Excelsior Wellness: Offers comprehensive behavioral health services, including specialized care for youth and young adults dealing with substance use disorders, accepting Medicaid and offering flexible payment options.
  • Chas Health: Provides integrated medical and behavioral health care at multiple clinics across the Spokane area. They accept almost all insurances, including Medicaid and Medicare, and offer a sliding fee discount program.
**Did you know?** Under Washington State law, Apple Health (Medicaid) covers outpatient substance use disorder treatment, including individual therapy and group counseling.

3. How to Choose the Right Therapist for Substance Abuse

Finding a therapist is a bit like dating—the first match isn't always the right one, and that's okay. The most important factor in therapy is the "therapeutic alliance" (how comfortable and understood you feel with your counselor).

  1. Look for specific credentials: In Washington state, look for a SUDP (Substance Use Disorder Professional) or a licensed mental health counselor (LMHC, LICSW) who explicitly lists "addiction" or "substance abuse" as a specialty.
  2. Ask about dual diagnosis: If you struggle with depression, anxiety, or bipolar disorder alongside addiction, you *must* treat both simultaneously. Ask potential therapists if they have experience with "co-occurring disorders."
  3. Do a consultation call: Most private practice therapists in Spokane offer a free 15-minute phone consultation. Use this time to ask: "What is your approach to addiction?", "Do you require abstinence, or do you practice harm reduction?", and "Do you have experience working with [your specific substance]?"

4. Outpatient vs. Inpatient Therapy

Therapy happens in different settings depending on the severity of the addiction:

  • Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP): This involves several hours of group and individual therapy per week. You still live at home and can often continue working. It provides a high level of support without requiring you to leave your life behind.
  • Inpatient / Residential Treatment: You live at a facility for 30, 60, or 90 days. Therapy is intensive and happens every day. This is recommended for severe dependencies or when outpatient treatment hasn't been successful.
  • Individual Outpatient Therapy: Standard therapy where you see a counselor for 45-60 minutes, once a week or every other week. This is great for long-term maintenance after completing an IOP or residential program.
