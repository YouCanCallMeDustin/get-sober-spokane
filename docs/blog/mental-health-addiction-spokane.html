Mental Health & Addiction in Spokane: Understanding Co-Occurring Disorders

Navigating recovery when you are dealing with both substance use and mental health challenges.

March 2026  ·  8 min read

For many people in Spokane, addiction doesn't exist in a vacuum. It is often deeply intertwined with mental health conditions like depression, anxiety, PTSD, or bipolar disorder. When someone struggles with both a substance use disorder and a mental health disorder, it is referred to as a **Dual Diagnosis** or **Co-Occurring Disorders**.

Which Comes First?

This is the "chicken or the egg" of the recovery world. Sometimes, an underlying mental health condition leads someone to "self-medicate" with drugs or alcohol to numb the pain. Other times, heavy substance use can trigger or worsen mental health symptoms.

Regardless of which started first, the reality is that they must be treated **simultaneously**. Treating the addiction while ignoring the underlying depression often leads to a quick relapse because the original driver of the behavior remains unaddressed.

Dual Diagnosis Resources in Spokane

Spokane has several facilities that specialize in "integrated treatment"—where mental health professionals and addiction counselors work together on a single plan.

  • Frontier Behavioral Health: The region's primary resource for community mental health, offering specific programs for individuals with co-occurring disorders.
  • CHAS Health: Provides integrated primary care, behavioral health, and substance use treatment under one roof, which is ideal for managing multiple prescriptions and therapies.
  • Native Project: Focuses on holistic wellness, providing integrated services for both youth and adults in the Spokane community.

Finding Support in Spokane

Traditional AA or NA meetings are incredible, but some people with dual diagnoses find specialized groups more comfortable.

  • Dual Recovery Anonymous (DRA): A 12-step based self-help group for people with a dual diagnosis.
  • NAMI Spokane: The local chapter of the National Alliance on Mental Illness offers support groups for families and individuals navigating mental health challenges.

You Are Not Alone

Over 50% of people with a substance use disorder also experience a mental health condition. We created our directory to help you find the specific help you need in Spokane.
