How to Help a Loved One with Addiction in Spokane

Addiction is a family disease. Here is how you can help them—and protect yourself.

March 2026  ·  10 min read

It's the most painful experience a person can go through: watching someone you love slowly disappear into addiction. In Spokane, a community heavily impacted by the opioid and meth crises, thousands of families are currently looking for a way out. But helping someone with an addiction is rarely as simple as asking them to stop; it requires a complex mix of boundaries, education, and local resources.

Step 1: Set Loving Boundaries

Helping is not the same as enabling. Enabling is doing something for the person that they can and should do for themselves, or protecting them from the natural consequences of their use.

  • Enabling Example: Paying their rent so they don't get evicted after spending their money on drugs.
  • Helping Example: Driving them to a treatment center when they are ready to go.

Boundaries aren't meant to punish the addict; they are meant to protect **your** sanity and financial stability.

Local Family Support Groups in Spokane

You cannot do this alone. Finding a community of people who understand your experience is vital.

  • Al-Anon / Nar-Anon: Spokane has dozens of weekly meetings for family members and friends of alcoholics and addicts. These groups focus on your recovery, regardless of whether your loved one gets sober.
  • Parent 2 Parent (P2P): A local support network specifically for parents whose children are struggling with substance use.
  • Frontier Behavioral Health Family Support: Offers counseling and education for families navigating the mental health and addiction system.

How to Talk About Treatment

Timing is everything. Avoid having serious conversations when the person is high or in the mid-withdrawal "panick." Instead, wait for a "moment of clarity"—usually when they are experiencing a negative consequence like a job loss or a health scare. Use "I" statements: *"I feel scared when I see you like this,"* rather than *"You are ruining our lives."*
